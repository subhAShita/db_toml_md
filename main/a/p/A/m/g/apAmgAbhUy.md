<details><summary>Text</summary>

अपाङ्गा भूयांसो यद्-उपरि, परं ब्रह्म तद्-अभूद्,  
अमी यत्र द्वित्राः, स च शत-मखादिस्, तद्-अधरात् ।  
अतः श्रीर्! *आम्नायस्* तद्-उभयम् उशंस् त्वां *प्रणिजगौ*  
प्रशस्तिः सा राज्ञो यद् अपि च पुरी-कोश-कथनम् ॥
</details>



<details><summary>शङ्करः (English)</summary>

That became परब्रह्म on which your (Vishnu's) copious side-glances fell. They became Indra and others on whom merely two or three such side-glances fell. And then the Vedas became your riches (Vedas) which praise you by way of praising those two (i.e., परब्रह्म and इन्द्रादि). Even an account of the city and the treasury is (in a roundabout way) the king's eulogy
</details>
