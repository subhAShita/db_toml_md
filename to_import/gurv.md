<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

गुर्व्-आज्ञाम् अधिमौलि येन सुमनो-मालां नवां **बिभ्रता**  
निर्माल्यं **विहितं** त्रि-लोक-शिरसां लङ्का-पतेः **शासनम्**।+++(5)+++  
स प्रेमार्द्र-विदेह-राज-तनया--सौमित्र-सेवा-**सुखी**  
देवो राघव-[सूनु]र् एव भगवान् **भूयाद् विभूत्यै** हरिः॥२  
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

गुर्वाज्ञाम् अधिमौलि येन सुमनोमालां नवां बिभ्रता  
निर्माल्यं विहितं त्रिलोकशिरसां लङ्कापतेः शासनम्।  
स प्रेमार्द्रविदेहराजतनयासौमित्रसेवासुखी  
देवो राघव[सूनु]रेव भगवान् भूयाद् विभूत्यै हरिः॥२  
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

Verse 2:  
He rendered the rule of Rāvaṇa (the lord of Laṅkā) on the heads of the three worlds  
akin to a garland that is fit to be discarded by himself,  
bearing the command of his father like a fresh garland of flowers over his head.+++(5)+++  
He is delighted by the service of Sītā (the daughter of the king of Videha) and Lakṣmaṇa (the son of Sumitrā). May that divine Lord, the scion of Raghu’s clan, Hari himself, bestow prosperity (on us).  
Note: Alternatively, Rāghavaketu, Rāghavabandhu, and Rāghavamauli also fit the fourth line of the verse.  
</details>
